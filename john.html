VIDEO GAME DESIGN AND DEVELOPMENT

Massively multiplayer online game (MMO or MMOG for short) is a video game in which many players are able to play simultaneously. Most games of this type only work on computers connected to the Internet, although there are also various games of this type that run on local computer networks. In most games of this type there is usually only one stage (or "world") in which all players take part. These games are made for all popular game platforms - PC, video game consoles, or smartphones. MMO games allow players to compete with each other, or take part in a large-scale collaboration, and sometimes even interact while playing with different people from around the world.



Sub-genres of multiplayer network games



  • MMORPG - In games in this subgenre the player usually chooses a character with supernatural powers. These games usually have several types of player characters, such as vampires, werewolves, or wizards. Examples of notable games of this type: World of Warcraft, Ragnarok, Final Fantasy XIV, and Ronescape.

    •

  • MMOFPS - In games in this subgenre the player usually chooses a soldier or mercenary, and is able to use many weapons, such as rifles and bombs, against the other players. Examples of notable games of this type: Battlefield Play4Free, PlanetSide 2, World War II Online, and Firefall.

    •

  • MMOSG - In games in this subgenre the player is focused on interacting with other users. These interactions include tours of virtual environments, communication via text or voice, and the like. Examples of notable games of this type: Second Life, and Club Penguin.

    •

  • MMOBG - These games do not require any installation - you can play them instantly through the web browser. Examples of notable games of this type: War Tribes, Hattrick, and OGame.
